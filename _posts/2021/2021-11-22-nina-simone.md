Eunice Kathleen Waymon—better known as Nina Simone—was a classically trained pianist turned genre-defying icon, weaving together blues, jazz, gospel, and pop with uncompromising political voice. Her work is raw, elegant, and often urgent.

A lifelong civil rights activist, Simone used her music to speak directly to the social realities of her time. These two tracks are essential listening—both culturally and musically.

[Mississippi Goddam (Live at Carnegie Hall, 1964)](https://youtu.be/4tHYGfRot5w)  
Her explosive response to the 1963 Birmingham church bombing.

[Feeling Good (1965)](https://youtu.be/oHRNrgDIJfo)  
A definitive, defiant version—equal parts anguish and liberation.
